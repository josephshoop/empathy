Build an Empathy App

Exploring human connection through psychology, philosophy, game theory, and interactive system design.

The Curriculum

Before building any application, we must understand what empathy actually is—a biological imperative, a phenomenological bridge, and a malleable skill.

1. Game Theory & Cooperation

Understanding reciprocal altruism and non-zero-sum games via works like The Evolution of Cooperation (Axelrod) and The Age of Empathy (de Waal).

2. Philosophy of Empathy

Exploring the epistemology of understanding "the other" through Edith Stein and Jamil Zaki's The War for Kindness.

3. Narrative & Fiction

Using narrative to transport the mind, referencing visceral empathetic literature like Octavia Butler's Kindred.

4. Game Design

Designing rulesets that elicit specific emotional responses, invoking Jesse Schell's Lens of Emotion.

Interactive Architectures

Synthesizing academic theory by designing platform-agnostic "Empathy Modules" that model and test emotional connection.

Module 1: The Interactive Dilemma

Demonstrates the mathematics of cooperation through a gamified social dilemma, pitting players against programmed automated strategies.

Module 2: The Perspective Engine

A narrative branching tree simulating structural constraints outside the user's lived experience, exposing cognitive phenomena like the Bystander Effect.

Module 3: The Reflection Dashboard

Visualizes the user's longitudinal "Empathy Index" across multiple sessions, treating connection as a consistently developing muscle.
